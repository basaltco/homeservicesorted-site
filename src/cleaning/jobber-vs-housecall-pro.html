Jobber vs Housecall Pro for Cleaning Businesses (2026)

Both tools are used by thousands of US and Canadian cleaning businesses. Both start at $49/mo. Both offer a 14-day free trial. The decision between them comes down to one question: is growing your Google review count your primary growth strategy, or do you want the most complete all-round job management platform?

Quick Verdict

Choose Housecall Pro if growing Google reviews is your primary growth strategy. The automated follow-up text sequences and review request automation are better than Jobber's equivalents — and for residential cleaning businesses where 5-star reviews drive most new client acquisition, that feature has a direct line to revenue.

Choose Jobber if you want the most complete job management platform — scheduling, quoting, online booking, client self-service portal, invoicing, and payments under one roof. Jobber has better Android parity, a stronger client portal, and works identically for US and Canadian cleaning businesses across every cleaning niche.

At the team plan level, Jobber is $10/month cheaper than Housecall Pro ($119 vs $129). At the top tier, Housecall Pro's Max plan is unlimited users while Jobber's Grow plan caps at 15 — so for larger teams, Housecall Pro becomes cost-competitive.

Key pricing detail: the features that make Housecall Pro distinctive for cleaning businesses — review automation, follow-up sequences — are on the Essentials plan ($129/mo), not Basic. How Each Tool Works on a Real Cleaning Job

Jobber

Client books through your website booking widget. Job goes into schedule. Cleaner gets notified via app (works on Android and iOS equally). Job completion triggers automatic invoice. Client pays through the self-service portal — no calls needed. Client can also log in to view all previous visits, approve quotes for future work, or reschedule. Property managers or commercial clients use the portal as their daily interface.

Housecall Pro

Client books online or you add them manually. Job goes into schedule with GPS dispatch. After the cleaning, automated thank-you text goes out. 24 hours later: automated review request, direct link to your Google Business profile. If no review is left, a follow-up text sends. Separately, you can mail physical postcards to your client list at any time — a campaign that consistently reactivates lapsed clients. For a 100-job-per-month operation, this runs 100 review requests automatically, every month, without manual involvement.

Frequently Asked Questions

Jobber vs Housecall Pro — which is better for a cleaning business?

It depends on what you value most. If growing Google reviews is a priority, Housecall Pro's automated review requests and follow-up sequences are better. If you want a more complete job management platform — client portal, better Android app, stronger quoting — Jobber wins. Both start at $49/mo and offer 14-day free trials, so you can test both before committing.

Does either tool work in Canada?

Yes — both Jobber and Housecall Pro serve US and Canadian cleaning businesses. Jobber is headquartered in Edmonton, Alberta, and has particularly strong Canadian market presence. Both platforms work identically on either side of the border; pricing is in USD.

Which is cheaper, Jobber or Housecall Pro?

They're very similar. At the team plan level, Jobber Connect ($119/mo, up to 5 users) is $10/month less than Housecall Pro Essentials ($129/mo, up to 5 users). At the top tier, Housecall Pro Max ($249/mo) allows unlimited users while Jobber Grow ($199/mo) caps at 15 users — so for larger teams, Housecall Pro is better value despite the higher price. Annual billing discounts are available on both platforms and reduce monthly cost.

Does Housecall Pro have a client portal?

Housecall Pro has basic client-facing features — clients can receive booking confirmations, automated texts, and invoices. It doesn't have the same depth of self-service portal that Jobber offers. In Jobber, clients get a full login where they can view all past and upcoming jobs, approve quotes, pay invoices, and manage their account independently. For property managers or commercial clients who want self-service visibility, Jobber's portal is the stronger choice.
