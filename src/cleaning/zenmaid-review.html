Quick Verdict

ZenMaid is the right tool for a residential maid service under 15 cleaners that wants cleaning-specialist features at the lowest price in the category. Tip tracking, recurring client profiles with room-level notes, and visit reminders are native features — not workarounds bolted onto a general field service tool. At $19/mo plus $4 per active cleaner, it's the most affordable cleaning-specialist option available.

The payment processing issue is the catch that prevents a higher rating. Multiple Capterra reviewers document failed transactions, duplicate charges, and delays. This isn't a minor UX complaint — it's a revenue collection problem. If collecting payment through the app is central to your workflow, test the payment processing carefully during the 14-day trial before committing. If it works for you, ZenMaid is excellent. If you hit problems, Jobber's payment processing is more reliable.

What ZenMaid Actually Does

ZenMaid was built specifically for residential maid service businesses — unlike Jobber, Housecall Pro, or Swept, which serve multiple home service verticals. That focus shows in the feature set.

The recurring client profile is the standout feature. Each client has a profile that stores not just contact details and scheduling preferences, but room-specific cleaning notes — "skip the home office on Tuesdays," "client uses only Method products," "front door code is 4892." Each cleaner sees these notes on the mobile app when they open their assignment. There's no equivalent in Jobber or Housecall Pro at this level of cleaning-specific detail.

Tip tracking is built in — cleaners can log tips received at each job, which rolls up into payroll reporting. Visit reminders go to clients automatically before each cleaning. The booking form integrates with your website so new clients can request cleanings online. These aren't remarkable features individually — but the fact that they're all native, all in one place, and all priced at under $40/mo for a small team is genuinely unusual.

How It Works on a Real Cleaning Job

A regular client — bi-weekly residential cleaning — has a profile that stores: cleaning preferences by room, which areas to skip on which visits, access instructions, products the client provides, and any household-specific notes (pets, alarms, parking). This profile follows the job regardless of which cleaner is assigned.

The morning of the cleaning, the assigned cleaner opens ZenMaid's mobile app and sees their schedule. Each job shows the client profile notes, a cleaning checklist, and the previous visit's completion notes. After the cleaning, the cleaner marks the job done, logs any tip received, and optionally takes completion photos. The client gets a notification that the cleaning is complete.

Twenty-four hours before the next visit, ZenMaid sends the client an automatic reminder. On a maid service with 50 recurring clients, that's 50 automated reminders going out on the right day, for the right visit, without any manual intervention.

The Catch

Payment processing is the biggest concern. Multiple Capterra reviewers report real problems: failed transactions, clients being charged twice, delays in funds reaching the business bank account. This isn't a single outlier complaint — it's a pattern documented across independent reviewers. If collecting payment through ZenMaid is core to your workflow, run the payment features thoroughly during the 14-day trial before you commit. Test a real transaction, not just the UI.

System crashes during updates. Several users report the app becoming temporarily unusable when ZenMaid pushes updates. This is less critical than the payment issue but worth knowing if you're counting on the app being available during a full day of scheduled cleanings.

Email support only — no phone. If you hit a payment processing problem mid-week and need urgent help, you're waiting on email. For a billing-critical issue, that gap is real. Jobber and Housecall Pro offer phone support on their standard plans.

Per-seat pricing scales past Jobber at larger team sizes. ZenMaid stays cheaper through approximately 15 cleaners. Beyond that, Jobber's Grow plan at $199/mo (up to 15 users) is a comparable cost for significantly more features. If you're growing, model the ZenMaid vs Jobber cost for your projected team size before you commit.

Who This Is NOT For

Anyone who needs reliable in-app payment collection. If your workflow depends on clients paying through ZenMaid's built-in payment processing, the documented issues are a real risk. Use Jobber or Housecall Pro, which have more reliable payment processing and phone support to resolve issues when they arise.

Commercial janitorial operations. ZenMaid is designed for residential maid services. Commercial cleaning with multiple building locations, inspection logs, and multilingual crews should use Swept instead.

Growing businesses planning to add other home services. If you're planning to add HVAC, landscaping, or other services alongside cleaning, Jobber handles all of them under one account. ZenMaid doesn't.

Teams growing past 15 cleaners. At that scale, ZenMaid's per-seat cost ($19 + $4×15 = ~$79/mo) approaches Jobber's Grow plan ($199/mo for up to 15 users), which offers significantly more functionality. Factor in whether the feature difference justifies the cost difference at your projected team size.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is ZenMaid worth it for a small maid service?

For a residential maid service under 10 cleaners that wants cleaning-specific features at the lowest price, yes. The recurring client profiles, tip tracking, and automated visit reminders are well-executed and better than what Jobber or Housecall Pro offer at this price point. The main thing to evaluate during your trial is the payment processing — it's documented as a real issue for some users, and it's worth testing with a real transaction before you commit.

What are the payment processing problems with ZenMaid?

Multiple Capterra reviewers document three categories of issues: failed transactions (clients who can't pay), duplicate charges (clients charged twice), and delays in funds being deposited. ZenMaid processes payments through a third-party processor, and the failure pattern seems to occur most often during periods of high transaction volume or after software updates. The company resolves issues via email support, which means delays if you hit a problem on a busy Friday. If reliable payment collection is critical, Jobber or Housecall Pro's payment processing is more consistent based on user reviews.

How does ZenMaid pricing work?

ZenMaid charges a base rate of $19/mo plus $4 per active cleaner per month. An "active cleaner" is one who has been assigned at least one job that month — inactive cleaners don't incur the per-seat charge. A 5-cleaner team costs approximately $39/mo. A 10-cleaner team costs approximately $59/mo. Annual billing is available and may reduce the monthly cost — verify current pricing at zenmaid.com.

Does ZenMaid have a client portal?

ZenMaid has a booking form for your website so new clients can request appointments. It doesn't have a full self-service client portal — clients can't log in to view their service history, approve quotes, or manage their own bookings the way Jobber's client portal allows. For a simple residential maid service, this is fine. For commercial clients or property managers who want visibility and self-service access, Jobber's portal is a significant advantage.

When should I switch from ZenMaid to Jobber?

Four clear signals: (1) you need online booking — Jobber's booking widget is more capable than ZenMaid's form; (2) your payment processing is causing problems during the trial; (3) you're adding services beyond maid cleaning and need one tool for all of them; (4) you have property manager or commercial clients who want a self-service portal. Price alone is not a reason to switch — ZenMaid stays cheaper through about 15 cleaners. See the full ZenMaid vs Jobber comparison.
