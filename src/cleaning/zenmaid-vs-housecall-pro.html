ZenMaid vs Housecall Pro: Which Is Better for Cleaning Businesses? (2026)

ZenMaid is built specifically for residential maid services — lower price, cleaning-native features like tip tracking and room-level client notes. Housecall Pro is a full-service platform with the best marketing automation in this category — automated review requests, follow-up texts, postcard campaigns. The question is whether cleaning-specialist features or customer communication automation matters more to your growth.

Quick Verdict

Use ZenMaid if you run a small-to-mid residential maid service (under 15 cleaners), want cleaning-specific features at the lowest price, and growing Google reviews isn't your primary acquisition channel. ZenMaid is dramatically cheaper for small teams.

Use Housecall Pro if growing Google reviews is your top growth strategy, you want automated customer follow-up sequences, your team is primarily on iOS, and you can absorb higher monthly costs as the business grows. Housecall Pro's marketing automation earns back its cost through client acquisition and retention — if you actually use it.

The Core Tradeoff

ZenMaid wins on price and cleaning-specific features. Housecall Pro wins on marketing automation and customer communication. The decision comes down to where your next client is coming from.

If most of your new clients come from Google reviews and word-of-mouth — which is true for most residential cleaning businesses — Housecall Pro's review automation is a direct growth lever. It runs independently once configured. Cleaning businesses that consistently run review campaigns report tripling their Google review count within months, which compounds into better local search visibility.

If your client base is mostly referrals and you're growing by adding cleaners to service existing demand, ZenMaid handles that growth at $4/cleaner per month. The operational difference between ZenMaid and Housecall Pro for day-to-day scheduling is small. The cost difference is not.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is ZenMaid or Housecall Pro better for a small cleaning business?

ZenMaid for a small residential maid service on a tight budget — it's dramatically cheaper and has cleaning-native features that Housecall Pro doesn't match. Housecall Pro for a cleaning business where growing Google reviews is the primary growth strategy — the automated review requests and follow-up sequences are built around that workflow. If you're genuinely unsure, start with ZenMaid's 14-day trial and test the payment processing carefully. If that works, it's the right choice at the lower price.

How much cheaper is ZenMaid than Housecall Pro?

For a small team, significantly cheaper. At 5 cleaners: ZenMaid ~$39/mo vs Housecall Pro Essentials $129/mo — a $90/month ($1,080/year) difference. Note that Housecall Pro Basic ($49/mo) doesn't include the review automation — you need Essentials at $129/mo for the features that distinguish Housecall Pro for cleaning businesses. If you're comparing the tools at their feature-relevant price points, the gap is even wider than it appears at first glance.

Does ZenMaid have automated review requests like Housecall Pro?

No — automated review requests and follow-up text sequences are Housecall Pro's standout feature and ZenMaid doesn't offer an equivalent. ZenMaid sends automatic visit reminders to clients but doesn't have the post-job review request automation or the sophisticated follow-up sequences that Housecall Pro uses for review generation. If automated review campaigns are important to your growth strategy, Housecall Pro wins this comparison.

Which tool is better if my team uses Android?

ZenMaid — or Jobber. Housecall Pro's Android app is rated approximately 3.3/5 and is the most consistent complaint from long-term users with Android-using cleaners. ZenMaid's Android app performs comparably to its iOS version. If any of your cleaners are primarily on Android, Housecall Pro is a real operational risk.
