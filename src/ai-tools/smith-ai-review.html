Quick Verdict

Smith.ai is the strongest standalone AI receptionist for home service businesses that aren't locked into one job management platform. The $97.50/mo AI-only plan handles 50 inbound calls per month, covers web chat, and books into whatever calendar or software you already use — no switching required. The live backup tier ($292.50/mo) adds trained human receptionists for calls the AI can't handle. It is the right choice for any business missing calls during jobs who wants a standalone solution that works regardless of whether they're on Jobber, Housecall Pro, or a Google Calendar.

The catch that matters most: the AI-only tier struggles with complex, multi-step calls. And call overage at $2.40/call adds up fast in a busy month. If you're already on Jobber, compare this with the Jobber AI Receptionist ($99/mo flat, no overage) before committing.

What Smith.ai Does

Smith.ai acts as your virtual front desk — it picks up calls your staff would otherwise miss and handles the interaction from greeting to booking confirmation. Here is what the AI handles:

  • Answers inbound calls within a few seconds, 24/7 — weekends and evenings included
  • Introduces itself using your business name and a receptionist name you choose
  • Collects caller name, phone number, service needed, job location, and preferred timing
  • Checks your calendar for available slots and books the appointment
  • Confirms the booking with the caller via text
  • Sends you a written summary of every call via email or app notification
  • Texts back callers who hang up before completing the booking — captures leads that would otherwise be lost
  • Handles web chat from your website with the same booking flow as voice calls

How It Works in Practice

You're finishing a kitchen drain job at 2 p.m. Your phone rings — a new caller asking about move-out cleaning for next Friday. Smith.ai answers within a few seconds, introduces itself as your virtual receptionist, collects the caller's name, service address, and preferred time slot, checks your calendar for availability, confirms the booking, and sends the caller a text confirmation. You get a summary notification while you're still packing up your tools. When you're in the truck, the job is in your calendar — no voicemail to check, no callback required, no data entry.

For existing clients who call back, Smith.ai handles the same flow — it does not recognize existing clients by phone number the way Jobber's AI Receptionist does, but it collects their details and books them without any friction.

The Catch

1. Overage costs can double your bill in a busy month. The $97.50 base covers 50 calls. A cleaning business handling 80–100 inbound calls per month pays $169–$217/mo on the AI-only plan — and that's before web chat. If your call volume is unpredictable, the Jobber AI Receptionist's flat $99/mo (no overage) is a safer option if you're on Jobber.

2. The AI-only tier struggles with complex, multi-step calls. A caller who wants to reschedule a recurring cleaning, add a second address, and ask about a billing issue in the same call will confuse the AI. It falls back to taking a message and requesting a callback — appropriate, but not the same as a human handling it. The live backup tier at $292.50/mo solves this.

3. No native integration with Jobber or Housecall Pro. Smith.ai books into Google Calendar natively. If you want bookings to appear as job records in Jobber or Housecall Pro, you need a Zapier connection or webhook — additional setup and potentially additional cost. If tight Jobber integration matters, the Jobber AI Receptionist is the cleaner path.

4. Some callers push back on AI. The AI voice is natural but identifiable as AI to attentive callers. For most home service calls — routine bookings, service enquiries, scheduling — this is not an issue. For high-end residential clients or commercial contract managers who expect a person on the first call, consider the live backup tier or Ruby.

Who This Is NOT For

  • Businesses already on Jobber — the Jobber AI Receptionist ($99/mo flat, no overage) books directly into your Jobber schedule without any integration setup. Smith.ai requires a Zapier connection to get bookings into Jobber. Compare both before choosing.
  • Very small operators handling under 20 inbound calls per month — at that volume, a missed call is one you could return within the hour. The ROI requires missing and recovering enough calls to justify $97.50/mo.
  • Businesses where every call needs a human — if your clients specifically expect a person, or your calls involve complex negotiations, Ruby's human-backed model is the right starting point.
  • Businesses outside the US and Canada — Smith.ai serves US and Canadian markets. Verify coverage on smith.ai if your business operates elsewhere.

What Users Report

Independent reviewers on Capterra and G2 consistently describe Smith.ai setup as fast — most operators report the receptionist live within one business day. The lead summary notifications are a recurring positive: operators describe checking call summaries between jobs the same way they check texts, rather than listening to voicemail stacks.

The most common complaint across independent reviews is overage billing — operators in busy seasons describe bills significantly above the base rate due to call volume exceeding the 50-call monthly allowance. Multiple reviewers recommend starting with a call volume audit before committing to a plan.

AI handling of complex calls is the second most-cited limitation — operators describe the AI falling back to message-taking for multi-step requests, which is the correct behavior but frustrating for callers expecting resolution on the first call. Reviewers who upgraded to the live backup tier describe significantly fewer complaints about call handling quality.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Smith.ai work with Jobber, Housecall Pro, or other scheduling software?

Smith.ai books into Google Calendar natively. For Jobber or Housecall Pro, you connect via Zapier to push booking data into your job management software — this requires a Zapier account and some setup time. If seamless Jobber integration without any additional tools is the priority, the Jobber AI Receptionist handles this natively at $99/mo flat. Smith.ai's advantage is that it works across any platform without requiring you to be on a specific job management software.

What happens when the AI can't handle the call?

On the AI-only plan, Smith.ai takes a message, asks for a callback number, and sends you a notification. The AI explains it cannot complete the request and follows up by text. On the live backup plan, the call escalates to a human receptionist in real time. For high-value service businesses where a botched call costs more than a month's subscription, the escalation path matters — confirm which tier you need before signing up.

Is Smith.ai the same as a virtual receptionist service?

The AI-only plan ($97.50/mo) is fully automated — no human agents involved. The live backup plan ($292.50/mo) adds trained human receptionists who handle calls the AI cannot manage. "Virtual receptionist service" typically refers to a service with human agents throughout; Smith.ai's AI-only tier is a different product category. If you specifically need a human answering every call, Ruby or Smith.ai's live backup tier is the right fit.

Can Smith.ai handle emergency calls?

For trades businesses that handle plumbing emergencies or HVAC breakdowns, Smith.ai can be configured to flag urgent calls and route them as a direct transfer to your mobile or to a human agent. Configure call routing rules in your account settings during onboarding. Verify current emergency routing capabilities on smith.ai before committing if this is a core requirement for your business.

Does Smith.ai handle Spanish-speaking callers?

Smith.ai handles calls in multiple languages, including Spanish. For home service businesses in markets with large Spanish-speaking residential populations, this is a meaningful capability. Verify current language support and any additional cost on smith.ai.

What is Smith.ai's cancellation policy?

Smith.ai offers monthly subscription plans. Verify current cancellation terms, any notice periods, and contract lengths on smith.ai before committing — these details change. There is no confirmed multi-year lock-in for standard subscription tiers, but confirm the current policy directly.
